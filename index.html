Wilson Financial Advisors has been offering comprehensive, fee-only financial planning to individuals and institutions since 1984.

Our Salt Lake City-based firm is composed of industry professionals with a strong background in investments, wealth management, tax planning, employee benefits and estate planning.

Fee-only

Fee-only compensation means an advisor never accepts commissions or compensation related to the products he or she recommends, and does not accept or pay referral fees. A fee-only compensation structure enables us to offer completely objective advice without any conflict of interest. Fee-only firms are dramatically different from fee-based advisory services, where an advisor charges fees and accepts commissions.

We offer clients an alternative to the commission or stockbroker world by delivering objective advice free of ulterior motives and conflicts of interest. We establish relationships with our clients through full disclosure and build lasting connections with education and communication. Our sole desire is to monitor, maintain and improve our clients’ financial health through sound investments and careful planning.

Fiduciary

As a member of the National Association of Personal Financial Advisors (NAPFA) we are required to adhere to the strictest ethical and educational standards in the financial services industry. NAPFA has stringent admission criteria and members commit to a fiduciary standard that ensures the client’s interest is always paramount.

A financial advisor held to a Fiduciary Standard occupies a position of special trust and confidence when working with a client. As a Fiduciary, the financial advisor is required to act with undivided loyalty to the client. This includes disclosure of how the financial advisor is to be compensated and any corresponding conflicts of interest.- NAPFA Board of Directors, Representatives and Financial Advisors

NAPFA Fiduciary Oath

  • The advisor shall exercise his/her best efforts to act in good faith and in the best interests of the client.
  • The advisor shall provide written disclosure to the client prior to the engagement of the advisor, and thereafter throughout the term of the engagement, of any conflicts of interest, which will or reasonably may compromise the impartiality or independence of the advisor.
  • The advisor, or any party in which the advisor has a financial interest, does not receive any compensation or other remuneration that is contingent on any client's purchase or sale of a financial product.
  • The advisor does not receive a fee or other compensation from another party based on the referral of a client or the client's business.
